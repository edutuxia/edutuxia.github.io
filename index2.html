Your Path To the UK

Your Path to the UK


Meet EduFind, our very smart AI tool. Provide some information about yourself and let EduFind do the hard work. Scanning through thousands of courses and hundreds of universities, EduFind will provide a shortlist of universities and courses that are personalised to your requirements and circumstances.

At this stage, you already have an idea about the type of courses that might be suitable for you. Our specialist advisors are also now armed with your profile and EduFind’s results. Book your free consultation with our advisors and tell us your story, your passion, aspirations, goals and interests.

Human expertise will now take over to develop your tailored programme. We will finalise your target universities based on your goals and ambitions and start working towards the ultimate career you want to build.

Your dedicated advisor will now work with you to define your roadmap for university applications. From English language tests to Statements of Purpose, your dashboard will clearly show all the steps needed to progress your application, and we will help you on every step of the way. Our dedicated application team will make and track the applications on your behalf so you can sit back and relax. Your dashboard will be updated as soon as an update on your applications is available.

At this stage, if you need Financial Assistance, our specialist advisors can help you find bank and other institutional loans.

This is a crucial step in your journey and one that must be handled with utmost care. We will guide you through the entire process of applying for your visa, including checking your application form, preparing/checking supporting documents, obtaining the Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) and preparing you for the visa interview.

Nearly there, but not quite yet! From finding suitable accommodation to opening a UK bank account to figuring out how to transfer money and booking flights and cabs - your advisor will ensure you do not lose your mental sanity in the chaos! Moving to the UK does NOT have to be a stressful experience, so let Edutuxia’s partners do the hard work for you. We have special partnerships with various service providers, making Edutuxia your true one-stop-shop.

Before you depart, you will also join in our pre-departure briefing, and receive a pre-departure crate of essentials to make sure you are all set up and roaring to go.

Some 8 hours of flying later, you have arrived in the UK. This country is about to transform your life. As an Edutuxian, you also automatically become a member of the National Indian Students and Alumni Union UK (NISAU). NISAU is the oldest and largest body representing Indian students and alumni in the UK and are there to look after your welfare and to help you with any problems you may have. They also host amazing events and activities throughout the year which you will be able to participate in.

Why Study in the UK?

The UK is home to many of the best universities in the world. 4 of the global top 10 universities are in the UK. A British education enables students to combine the world-leading learning and knowledge with real-world application, ensuring that you are becoming critical skills that start to prepare you for the professional world even while you are studying. The Quality Assurance Agency (QAA) reviews the courses all universities offer to maintain high standards of learning, research and teaching.

The research that is carried out by UK universities impacts lives in the UK and throughout the world - daily. Take the most recent phenomenal work done by Oxford university as an example - the development of the Oxford-Astrazeneca vaccine is the latest example of the finest infrastructure and technical expertise that is available at British universities. The UK is a knowledge economy, and well on its way to becoming the innovation economy of the world - this innovation starts in its universities, which you become a part of when you study in the UK.

British degrees are usually shorter in length - a masters degree is typically one year long while undergraduate degrees are three years in length. At all levels there is such a breadth of courses available that you can specialise in your chosen field from very early on. So whether you want to become an investment banker or a cybercrime specialist - there is a specialist and intensive course waiting for you. Cross-discipline courses are also available; these allow you to combine different subjects into your course, such as Product Engineering and Finance or Healthcare and Policymaking.

The UK has an overall atmosphere of excellent industry-academic links. Universities often work with local employers, and employers come onto campuses to reach out to students even before they graduate. There are activities and events enabling you to find and speak to employers throughout the year. Most universities have very good Careers Services that can help you with your job applications.

British degrees are very well-recognised all over the world, by global employers, universities and governments. A UK education enables the development of skills that employers want including critical thinking, effective communication and problem solving.

Graduates are eligible for a 2 years post-study work visa, or 3 years if you do a PhD. Graduates who find an appropriate job and meet the requirements will be able to switch into skilled work, which is a route to settlement in the UK.

An Office for Talent is being set up to enable scientific talent to be welcomed and prosper in the UK.

During term-time you can work up to 20 hours per week, and during holidays you can work full-time.

The UK provides a host of scholarships and financial support for aspiring students. So if you have a good academic score and plan in advance, you can find studying in the UK to be very affordable. Financial aid can be partially or fully paid. Scholarships and bursaries are available from various sources, such as the British Government, universities and various public and private organisations. Edutuxia can help you find and carefully apply for financial aid where eligible.

From the wonderful outdoors of the Scottish Highlands to the British landmarks and attractions that hold irreplaceable cultural, scientific and historical significance - immerse yourself in thousands of years of well-preserved history. Get yourself a cuppa in the cafe JK Rowling wrote the iconic Harry Potter in, enjoy the thrills of the Premier League in Manchester or of cricket at Lords, explore Shakespeare’s birthplace in Stratford-upon-Avon or shop till you drop in London’s Oxford Street - there is something incredible for every palette.

The UK is a vibrant democracy, welcoming and cherishing people of Indian origin! In 2021 Indians hold many top positions across sectors in the UK, whether it's their Finance Minister or their Home Minister. Chicken Tikka Masala is widely considered Britain’s official dish and with more than 1.5 million NRIs and PIOs - you will never feel too far from home! Many universities have Indian societies and institutes on campuses, and the National Indian Students and Alumni Union UK has its presence country-wide!

With Europe on its doorstep, explore France, Amsterdam, Italy, Spain and more - just a short flight away! Once the European Union, the world is your oyster, whether its wild parties in Ibiza or the serenity of Tuscany that floats your boat.
